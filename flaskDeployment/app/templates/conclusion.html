After optimizing and training our models, we assessed their performance in predicting test values of our dataset. The score comparison reveals Multilayer Perceptron and Polynomial Regression as the most accurate models, with Polynomial Regression slightly outperforming MLP.

Why we chose this topic

Healthcare is easily one of the most important industries in the world. If Healthcare access becomes increasingly restricted through insurance charges, it will negatively affect millions of people and compromise the future. If people are more aware of how much money may be going into their medicinal services, then it will be easier for them to overcome any financial difficulties.

Further Extensions

Despite the accuracy and precision of our model, the predictions we make are limited to the dataset that we sourced. The credibility of the dataframe we used for this project is unknown, which affects the validity of the product. The Insurance Charge Predictor would be more practical if the model could learn from real time patient insurance data. Additionally, the model could work better if the variety of features is increased. There are many different things in the real world that affect the final cost of health insurance, and our dataframe doesn't account for all of them.
